what does it mean to be perfect?

Posted on Fri, Mar 19, 2021 exploration
The law of the Lord is perfect, refreshing the soul.

The question was brought to mind when I saw that the footnote for the word 'perfect' also used the word 'blameless.'

Is this the idea of perfection, and that's why Jesus's sacrifice on the cross makes us perfect? That perfection isn't about having no mistake as much as it is about having no reason for blame.

  • It makes sense why the word says that there is no accusation in Christ.
  • A metaphor that can exemplify this: It's like going back to the concept of being a child, where a child does have his or her flaws, yet they are not blamed when they make a mistake. As an adult we can't bear to even blame them.
